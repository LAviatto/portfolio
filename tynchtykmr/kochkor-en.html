Kol-Ukok (Kochkor)

The small lake Kol-Ukok (Kel-Ukok) is located in the Naryn region, in the region of the northeastern part of the Terskey-Ala-Too mountain range. This is a mountain lake and it is very cold. People come here not for swimming, but to enjoy the peace and beauty.

In the waters of Kol-Ukok, the Svan trout, brought from Armenia, lives. In the meadows located next to the lake, you can find edelweiss. And in the vicinity of the reservoir there are wild animals: wolves, foxes, hares and mountain goats.

It is said that the caravans of the Great Silk Road passed through the Torugart Pass to the Chinese Kashgar.

Kol-Tor (Kegety)

The mountain lake Kel-Tor is located 86 km from the city of Bishkek on the northern slopes of the Kyrgyz Range. It is located at an altitude of 2725 meters above sea level. Here you can enjoy walks through the spruce forest and dense thickets of mountain ash, honeysuckle, wild rose, raspberries and barberries, which are subsequently replaced by juniper bushes and alpine meadows.

The lake itself was formed as a result of an earthquake and is a dam-type lake. The water in the lake has a beautiful turquoise hue.

The water temperature is not suitable for swimming, as it is quite cold and warms up to only 5 degrees in summer.

Hiking route to the lake about 8 kilometers one way. The journey will take 3-5 hours.
